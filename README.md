## About the Workshop

AGENT4SC focuses on the systems foundations required to operationalize autonomous AI agents in large-scale scientific environments. It addresses how agents that can reason, plan, and act across the edge–cloud–HPC continuum reshape computational and data-driven research — and the challenges this introduces for safety, reproducibility, observability, and resource management at scale.

## Key Topics

- System architectures and design principles for agentic AI beyond LLMs at scale
- Agentic AI in the edge–cloud–HPC continuum for cross-facility science
- Agent memory, planning, and long-horizon reasoning under resource constraints
- Data and metadata architectures (databases, vector stores, knowledge graphs)
- Human-in-the-loop, steering, and oversight for long agentic workflow runs
- Provenance, auditability, reproducibility, and observability at scale
- Safety, security, reliability, and accountability mechanisms
- Hallucination detection, failure mitigation, and recovery at scale
- Performance modeling for agentic systems
- Interoperability and standardization of agent interfaces and protocols
- Cross-platform scheduling and resource awareness under agent-driven control
- Experience reports from real-world large-scale scientific deployments
